Demo Scenarios - Text Wrapping - `text-wrap: wrap;` CSS Property

Scenario 1 - Analyze Default Behavior of Unicode Line Breaking Algorithm - `text-wrap: wrap;`

Case 1 - Single Word fits within the container "Word"
Word
  • Description: "Word" fits within the container
  • - The text "Word" is a single word.
  • - The word fits within the container and is displayed on the same line without any overflow.
Case 2 - Single Word overflowing the container "Word2"
Word2
  • Description: "Word" fits within the container, but the last character '2' overflows the container.
  • - The text "Word2" is a single word.
  • - '2' is the character that exceeds the container's boundary, leading to overflow.
  • - Since, '2' belongs to Non-breaking character class
  • - So, "Word2" does not split or wrap to next line and is displayed on the same line.
  • - Conclusion: "Word2" is displayed on the same line with '2' overflowing the container.
Case 3 - Single Word overflowing the container "LongWord1"
LongWord1
  • Description: "Long" fits within the container, but the remaining part "Word1" overflows the container.
  • - The text "LongWord1" is a single word.
  • - 'W' is the character that exceeds the container's boundary, leading to overflow.
  • - Since, 'W' belongs to Non-breaking character class.
  • - So, "LongWord1" does not split or wrap to next line and is displayed on the same line.
  • - Conclusion: "LongWord1" is displayed on the same line with 'W' causing the overflow.
Case 4 - Multiple Spaces are Collapsed to Single Space "A          BC"
A BC
  • Description: Multiple spaces are collapsed to a single space character.
  • - The text "A          BC" consists of 3 parts.
  • - Part 1: "A" fits within the container.
  • - Part 2: 10 Space Characters are present between "A" and "BC".
  • - Part 3: "BC" at the end of the same line.
  • - CSS Implicitly applies `white-space: normal` or `white-space-collapse: collapse` by default to all elements.
  • - Due to this default behavior, multiple spaces are collapsed into a single space character.
  • - Multiple Spaces are removed from the DOM as well, and only a single space is retained.
  • - Conclusion: "A          BC" is displayed as "A BC" on the same line.
Case 5 - Multiple Words separated by Space "Word1 Word2 LongWord"
Word1 Word2 LongWord
  • Description: Multiple words separated by space.
  • - The container has a fixed width of 500px & the words are separated by space characters.
  • - The text "Word1 Word2 LongWord" consists of 3 words.
  • - Initial 2 words "Word1 Word2" fits within the container of width 500px and are displayed on the first line.
  • - But, the last word "LongWord" exceeds the container's boundary and overflows.

  • Let's analyze why "LongWord" wraps to the next line and does not overflow the container.
  • - "Lon" could easily fit within the first line of the container, but "gWord" would lead to overflow.
  • - Since, 'g' (Non-breaking character class) is the character that exceeds the container's boundary for the first line.
  • - And, the word "LongWord" occurs in the middle of the line, and does not start at the beginning of the line.
  • - So, the word "LongWord" wraps to the next line and does not overflow the container.
  • - If the word "LongWord" started at the beginning of the line, it would have overflowed the container.
  • - Consider the Case 6 for a similar scenario where a long word starts at the beginning of the line.
  • - Conclusion: "Word1 Word2" is displayed on the first line, and "LongWord" is wrapped to the next line.
Case 6 - Overflow of Long Word starting at the 2nd line "Word1 Word2 ABC LongWordWithoutAnySpace"
Word1 Word2 ABC LongWordWithoutAnySpace
  • Description: LongWordWithoutAnySpace wraps to the 2nd line and overflows the container without further splitting into the 3rd line.
  • - The container has a fixed width of 500px & the words are separated by space characters.
  • - The text "Word1 Word2 ABC LongWordWithoutAnySpace" consists of 4 words.
  • - Initial 3 words "Word1 Word2 ABC" fits within the container of width 500px and are displayed on the first line.
  • - ' ' (Space) after "ABC" is the character that exceeds the container's boundary for the first line.
  • - Space is a natural break point, it allows the word "LongWordWithoutAnySpace" to wrap on to the next line.
  • - So, "LongWordWithoutAnySpace" wraps to the 2nd line and does not overflow the container at the end of the first line.

  • Now let's analyze why "LongWordWithoutAnySpace" overflows the container in the 2nd line & doesn't split further into 3rd line.
  • - "LongWordWithoutAnySpace" starts at the beginning of the 2nd line.
  • - "LongWordWithout" fits within the container, but "AnySpace" overflows the container.
  • - Although, 'A' (Non-breaking character class) is the character that exceeds the container's boundary for the 2nd line.
  • - "LongWordWithoutAnySpace" does not further wrap or split in to the 3rd line, as it starts at the beginning of the 2nd line.
  • - It simply overflows the container at the end of the 2nd line and does not wrap to the 3rd line.
  • - Since, "LongWordWithoutAnySpace" is a single word starting at the beginning of the 2nd line.
  • - It behaves the same way like in the Case 3 where a Single Word overflows the container without wrapping or splitting to the next line.

  • Conclusion:
  • - "Word1 Word2 ABC" is displayed on the first line,
  • - "LongWordWithoutAnySpace" is wrapped to the 2nd line and overflows the container without further splitting into the 3rd line.

Word1 Word2 Word3
